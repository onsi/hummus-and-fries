# Ethnically Ambiguous Chicken and Rice

I came up with this the other day... seemed tasty enough to warrant adding!  I serve it with an aromatic rice but you can probably do plain rice if you want to keep it simple.  Would probably go well with naan or pita bread too.

## Ingredients

### Chicken, Marinade, and Sauce

- [ ] 1.5 lbs chicken breast (or thighs if you prefer)

#### Marinade

- [ ] 1 cup yoghurt
- [ ] 1/4 cup avocado oil
- [ ] juice of 2 lemons
- [ ] 8 cloves garlic
- [ ] 1/4 bunch parsley
- [ ] 3/4 tsp kosher salt
- [ ] 1/4 tsp black peppercorn
- [ ] 1/4 tsp white pepper
- [ ] 1 tsp cinnamon
- [ ] 1 tsp garlic powder
- [ ] 1 tsp onion powder
- [ ] 1 tsp garam masala
- [ ] 1/8-3/4 tsp red kashmiri chili powder (depending on how spice you want it)
- [ ] 2 Tbsp honey
- [ ] 2 Tbsp tomato paste

#### Sauce
- [ ] 1/2 cup milk (I use almond milk.  you can go with 1/4 cup cream instead if you'd like)
- [ ] 1/2 cup water
- [ ] 3 roma tomatoea, diced (or a can of diced tomatos, though you may want to adjust the amounf of liquid then).

### Rice

- [ ] 3 Tbsp olive oil
- [ ] 1 large red onion, diced
- [ ] 1/2 tsp cloves
- [ ] 1/2 cup frozen peas
- [ ] 1/2 cup frozen corn
- [ ] 1/2 cup diced carrots
- [ ] 1 can chickpeas, drained
- [ ] 2 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 1/3 cup pine nutes
- [ ] 3/4 tsp salt
- [ ] 1 tsp turmeric
- [ ] 1 tsp cinnamon
- [ ] 1/2 tsp black pepper
- [ ] 1.5 cups basmati rice, rinsed and drained
- [ ] 1 can chicken stock + enough water to bring it up to 3 1/4 C total liquid

## Method

### Marinate the Chicken

1. Place garlic and parsely leaves (not stems) in pestle with salt and black peppercorn.  Crush with mortar until a paste is formed.
2. Combine garlic paste with all other marinade ingredients and whisk together until fully combined.  I like to do this in a rectangular pyrex that I have a lid for.
3. Take each chicken breast and produce three pieces of roughly equal thickness.  I like to cut off the thin tail end (just below the half-way mark) and then take the remaining top of the breast and slice it through the center into two thin pieces.  (It's hard to describe this.  I simple mean: lay the half-breast on your cutting board, place your hand on it, rotate your kife so the flat of the knife is parallel to your counter top, then cut the chicken into two thin pieces - the top half facing you and bottom half facing the cutting board.)
4. Layer the chicken into the marinade and let marinate (for at least 30 minutes.  Up to 8 hours) in the fridge.

### Prepare the Rice

1. Cook up diced onion in oil, sprinkle with the black pepper as it cooks.
2. When onions are soft, add frozen peas, frozen corn, carrots, and chickpeas.  Sautee.
3. Add garlic, pine nuts, salt, turmeric, cinnamon, and cloves.  Sautee until aromatic (~30 seconds).  Add a bit more olive oil if need be.
4. Add rice and sautee for a couple of minutes.
5. Add liquid, bring to a boil, then cover and simmer for 17-20 minutes (the majority of the water should be gone when it's done).
6. Turn off heat, fluff the rice and let sit covered.

### Grill the chicken

1. Preheat grill and oil the grates.  Medium-high heat should be fine.
2. Take each piece of chicken out of the marinade and place on grill.  It should still be pretty much drenched in marinade.  Reserve remaining marinade.
3. Cook for 3-4 minutes with grill lid closed.
4. Flip chicken.  Cook for 2-3 minutes more with lid closed, until the thickest piece registers 165ºF with a meat thermometer.
5. Transfer cooked chicken to a cutting board and let rest for 5 minutes.
6. Slice perpendicular to the grain into thin slices.  Set aside.

### Prepare the sauce

1. Place marinade in pan.  Add water, milk/cream, and tomatoes.
2. Bring to a boil, stirring frequently, then let simmer.
3. Add sliced chicken and continue to simmer for 5 minutes.

Serve by plating rice, then spooning chicken + sauce over rice.
