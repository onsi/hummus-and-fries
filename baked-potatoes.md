# Baked Potatoes

Creamy interiors; crunchy exteriors.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 6 medium/large yukon gold potatoes
- [ ] 4 Tbsp butter, cold
- [ ] 1/4 cup olive oil
- [ ] 2 Tbsp cornmeal
- [ ] 1 Tbsp cornstarch
- [ ] 1.5 tsp garlic powder
- [ ] 1 tsp onion powder
- [ ] 1 tsp kosher salt
- [ ] ground black pepper

## Method

1. Preheat oven to 400ºF.  Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Wash, but do not peel, potatoes.  Using a pairing knife cut a 1" deep, ~1" wide, wedge out of the top of the potato.  Your goal is to form a V-shaped valley along the entire length of the top of the potato that the butter can sit in.  Save the wedges.
3. Using a fork, poke deep several holes into the exposed potato along the entire valley.  Don't skimp either on depth or number of pokes.  The goal is not just to allow steam to escape but to provide channels for the butter to enter into the potato as it melts.
4. Pour olive oil into a shallow boil; combine cornmeal, cornstartch, garlic powder, onion powder, and 1/2 tsp kosher salt in another shallow bowl.
5. Stick a fork in the middle of the potato valley so you can pick up the potato by the fork.  Dip and swirl the potato in the olive oil so that the skin is covered in oil.  Then dip and rotate the oily potato into the cornmeal mixture.  Your goal is to form a crust over the potato skin (but not the exposed valley).
6. Place the potato on the parchment paper, valley-side up.
7. Cut thin patties of butter and tuck them into the valley along both walls.  Drizzle olive oil on top of the butter.  Repeat for all potatos then sprinkle with black pepper and remaining salt.
8. Swirl the reserved wedges in any remaining oil and drench in any remaining cornmeal mixture (you might need to make a bit more to coat the wedges).  Place on tray.
9. Bake at 400ºF for 40 minutes.  Then raise temperature to 425ºF and bake for another 8-10 minutes.  A fork inserted into the potato should go through with no resistance.

Optionally, you can sprinkle on some shredded cheese (e.g. parmesan, sharp cheddar) for the last ~15 minutes.

I like to serve these with [chili](cheaters-chli.md), shredded cheddar, tomatoes, green onions, avocado, and labneh.
